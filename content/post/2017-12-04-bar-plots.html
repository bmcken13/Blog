I’m going to use team homerun totals for all the MLB teams in 1980 to create bar plot.

query<-"SELECT name,HR FROM Teams WHERE yearID=1980 ORDER BY HR "
result<-sqldf(query)
result$name<-factor(result$name,levels=result$name)

Notice something different? The last line is used for saving the results as a factor. Thus, the system knows that the order of the results should not be in alphabetical order. The last line is saving the information as a factor, and letting the system know that the order the results appear in the order we specified, not default alphabetical.

Once we have our data, it’s time to create a bar plot. Like histograms, scatterplots and time series - ggplot2 coding is a necessity.

ggplot()+
  geom_bar(data=result,aes(x=name,y=HR),stat='identity')+
  coord_flip()+
  ggtitle("1980 Team Homerun Totals")+
  xlab("Team Name")+
  ylab("Homeruns")
